# AI API Ops Taste Guide

## A · Ops Terminal style

AI API Ops should feel like an operations terminal for AI systems: dark, precise, calm, and credible.

## Allowed visual patterns

- Dark developer-ops palette
- Terminal-like hero treatment
- Compact card grids
- Monospace labels and operational badges
- Clear hierarchy with one dominant H1
- Small evidence-oriented callouts
- Minimal motion and restrained emphasis

## Forbidden visual patterns

- Generic SaaS landing-page styling
- Fake dashboards
- Noisy charts
- Heavy gradients
- Stock imagery
- Over-animated hero sections
- Keyword-stuffed hero copy
- Crowded first-screen layouts

## Homepage hero rules

- H1 must remain dominant.
- Hero copy must not overcrowd the first screen.
- Supporting subtitle copy must stay secondary.
- The terminal / ops panel must support the message, not compete with it.
- Homepage expansion must preserve the A · Ops Terminal identity.

## Article page rules

- Keep article pages readable and operational.
- Use concise summaries, quick answers, checklists, evidence blocks, and FAQs.
- Do not introduce decorative noise or gimmicks.
- Preserve clarity over visual novelty.

## Taste QA checklist

- H1 is the primary focal point
- Hero is uncrowded
- Subtitle does not overpower H1
- Terminal/ops framing is still obvious
- No generic SaaS feel
- No fake analytics visuals
- No unnecessary motion
- No design drift from the existing dark ops identity
